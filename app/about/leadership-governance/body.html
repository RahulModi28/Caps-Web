CAPS Policies

Care & responsibility

CAPS’s governance structures ensure the centre is guided with integrity, accountability and care. Our Advisory Board (below) and Executive Team (above) are responsible for upholding the policies, standards and support commitments that define our community.

CAPS Support Guidelines

General Policies & Guidelines

Advisory Board

The Advisory Board is our governing body, and provides the formal leadership to the strategic framework. The Advisory Board supports the Executive Team to ensure that the centre is well-managed and operates successfully. Its role is to govern the centre and not to be involved in its day-to-day management. It is the Leadership Team’s role to manage the centre in accordance with the strategic goals set by the Advisory Board and under its direction.

Frequently asked questions

‍

What is the Centre for Academic and Professional Support (CAPS)?

CAPS is a student-centric platform at CHRIST (Deemed to be University), Yeshwanthpur Campus, designed to facilitate academic excellence and professional readiness. We offer peer-to-peer mentoring, specialized workshops, writing support, and personal development opportunities to help students transition successfully into their professional careers.

What are the different wings of CAPS and what services do they offer?

CAPS operates through specialized wings to cover all aspects of student growth: One on One Peer Training offers individualized mentoring sessions focused on academic subjects, communication, and soft skills; Group Peer Training provides interactive group sessions, skill bootcamps, and exam preparation circles; Connect Wide drives extracurricular activities, university-wide events, and external community networking; and Research & Assessment supports student research initiatives, project guidance, and capability assessments.

How can I book a session or register for CAPS workshops?

Students can easily book a session by clicking the "BOOK A SESSION" button in the main navigation header or by visiting the CAPS portal directly. From the portal, you can select your preferred wing, choose a mentor, and schedule a convenient time slot.

How can I join CAPS as a student coordinator, mentor, or team lead?

CAPS recruits passionate student coordinators, mentors, and team leads at the start of each academic semester. Applications open university-wide, followed by a selection process based on academic proficiency, leadership skills, and peer coaching capability. Keep an eye on our announcements and social channels for application links.
