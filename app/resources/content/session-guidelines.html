Coaching Best Practices

CAPS maintains a professional and supportive peer-learning environment. Review our guidelines, check-lists, and cancellation policies to make the most of your peer training and coaching sessions.

Policy & Preparation

Read through each phase of preparation and execution to maximize the value of your interactions with CAPS peer trainers.

Before the Session
  • Upload Draft Materials: Submit your draft paper, prompt, data sheet, or project outline at least 24 hours prior to the session. This helps your trainer prepare customized feedback.
  • Be Punctual: Arrive 5 minutes before the scheduled slot at block B room 227 or connect to the virtual call link on time.
  • Clarify Goals: Have a specific question or topic in mind that you want to resolve during the 45-minute session.

During the Session
  • Collaborative Exchange: CAPS is a coaching center, not a writing or correction factory. Peer trainers guide you; they will not write your papers or solve assignments for you.
  • Active Participation: Bring a notebook or laptop, take notes, ask clarifying questions, and participate in exercises.
  • Maintain Mutual Respect: Engage professionally, respect the peer trainers' time, and value constructive critique.

Cancellation & No-Show Policy
  • Cancel in Advance: If you cannot make it, reschedule or cancel at least 12 hours prior. This allows other students on the waitlist to claim the slot.
  • Missed Sessions Policy: Three consecutive or non-consecutive no-shows without notification will result in a suspension of your booking privileges for the remainder of the semester.
  • Emergency Circumstances: If you miss a slot due to medical reasons or scheduled classes, contact the operations desk to reactivate your profile.
